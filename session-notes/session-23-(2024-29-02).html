The Vision of the Skywatch Aiudara

The aiudara stabilizes before you. In the shimmering blue of the portal light, you see a great pyramid of pitch-black stone far in the distance, upon which two large silver chains, each looking weak and worn, bind the exterior. Above the pyramid, a black sun hangs heavy in the sky, a ring of fire flickering from its edges like tentacles. The field before the pyramid is host to an uncountable number of humanoid bodies, each kneeling in the snow before the dark structure, appearing to bow to its majesty. The structure’s surface ripples like water, and a great chasm breaks upon it, revealing a massive ice-blue eye shot through with luminous purple veins. The kneeling figures stand, a black mist sloughing off their bodies. "Osoyo" echoes through your minds, and somewhere deep inside, you hear your own voice repeat the call. Sakuachi shines with a golden light in your sight, and it takes an immense amount of control to stop yourself from reaching for her, to stop yourself from snuffing out the hated light. A faint flicker on the surface of the Black Sun shows the Aqakaru, and a golden droplet of fire drips down from it. As it falls, it starts to flare out like a golden star, the light overtaking the pyramid. The scene through the portal changes, and Nishara, it almost seems like there’s a slim ivory hand in the star’s glare, guiding it across the sky of a now familiar landscape.

You’re heard tales of this place – seen the illustrations in journals and books. Perhaps you even have been there at some point, although doubtful, as you are all still alive. The falling star burns over the Worldwound Scar, in the demon-haunted wasteland of Sarkoris. Where the Abyss took countless lives, and a decade ago, a group of adventurers, commanders of the Last Crusade, slew the Demon Lord Deskari to stop the endless invasion at the cost of their souls. The sound of metal screaming overtakes your mind. A chain snaps on the black pyramid. The portal light dies off, and the dark voice in your soul screams out in the pain of loss. OSOYO it cries. WE ARE OSOYO. WE WILL BE FREE.
